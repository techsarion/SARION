# Sarion — Competitor Analysis & Positioning

## Phase 4: Competitor Analysis

| Competitor | Strengths | Weaknesses (for agencies) | Pricing (approx.) | Why agencies use it | Why agencies leave it |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **ClickUp** | Extremely powerful, flexible, cheap, all-in-one PM | Overwhelming, steep setup, no real CRM/invoicing, no clean client portal | ~$7–12/user/mo | Cheap, does "everything" for internal work | Complexity fatigue; can't give clients a clean view; no invoicing |
| **Monday.com** | Polished UI, easy boards, popular | Per-seat cost scales painfully; weak client portal; no native invoicing/CRM depth | ~$9–19/user/mo | Looks nice, easy team adoption | Gets expensive fast; not built for client-facing or billing |
| **HubSpot** | Best-in-class CRM, marketing/sales automation | Massively overkill + expensive for small agencies; not a delivery/PM/portal tool | Free tier → $$$$ ($800+/mo at scale) | Strong CRM/pipeline for those who grow into it | Price cliff; too sales-heavy; no project delivery or client portal |
| **Zoho CRM** | Cheap, feature-broad ecosystem | Clunky UX, fragmented across Zoho apps, dated feel | ~$14–40/user/mo | Budget CRM with lots of modules | UX frustration; needs many Zoho apps stitched together |
| **Trello** | Dead-simple, free, loved | Too basic; no CRM, invoicing, reporting, or client portal | Free → ~$5–10/user/mo | Easy task boards | Outgrown quickly; not a business system |
| **Notion** | Infinitely flexible, cheap, beautiful docs | You build everything yourself; no invoicing, weak client-facing portal, no automation | ~$8–15/user/mo | Customizable, replaces docs + light PM | Maintenance burden; breaks at scale; not purpose-built |

### Market gaps Sarion can exploit

1. **No incumbent is "agency-shaped" out of the box.** They're horizontal tools agencies *bend* into shape. Sarion's wedge = **opinionated, pre-built-for-agencies** (clients, projects, invoices, portal — already wired together).
2. **The client portal gap.** None of these offer a clean, branded, client-facing portal by default. This is the single most defensible differentiator.
3. **Per-seat pricing punishes agencies.** Your **flat per-account pricing** ($29/$59/$99) is a genuine wedge — say it loudly.
4. **CRM ↔ delivery ↔ invoicing are split across tools.** Owning the full "lead → project → invoice → paid" loop in one place is the unmet job.
5. **Simplicity for the 1–20 segment.** ClickUp/HubSpot are too much; Trello/Notion are too little. Sarion can own the *right-sized* middle.

## Phase 5: Positioning Strategy

**Unique Value Proposition**
> Sarion is the all-in-one CRM and client portal built specifically for small agencies — manage clients, track projects, and send invoices in one place, while giving your clients a professional portal that ends the endless status emails.

**Elevator Pitch**
> Most small agencies run on a messy stack of spreadsheets, Trello, and Stripe — nothing talks to each other, clients are confused, and invoices slip through the cracks. Sarion replaces that chaos with one agency-built system: clients, projects, and invoicing in a single dashboard, plus a branded client portal so your clients always know what's happening. Agencies save 5–10 hours a week and look more professional doing it — starting at $29/month flat, not per seat.

**One-Sentence Product Description**
> The all-in-one CRM and client portal made for small agencies.

**Landing Page Headline**
> **Run Your Entire Agency From One Place.**

**Landing Page Subheadline**
> Sarion combines client management, project tracking, and invoicing with a professional client portal — so you save 5–10 hours a week and your clients always know what's going on. Flat pricing from $29/month, no per-seat fees.
